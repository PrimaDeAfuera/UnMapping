Why UnMapping?

Unmapping is a practice of illuminating landscapes that are erased, un-chartable, and/or otherwise unmapped by contemporary and mainstream maps and mapping practices. This includes maps and methods used across various scholarly and political sectors like environmental planning, spatial analyses of energy equity, and cultural narratives of history and place. The map below, centered around the archipelago of Borinkén, lives alongside existing counter mapping projects that highlight the rich legacies of survivance of Afro and Indigenous peoples in the wake of the interconnected forces of climate change (ecocide) and racist settler-colonialism (and genocide) within the so-called US territories, in the Caribbean, and in imaginaries beyond.
